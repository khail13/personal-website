About Me

Hey!

I'm Khail (pronounced “kyle”), a 4th year Mechatronic Systems Engineering student at Simon Fraser University. As a child, I remember watching in awe the engineering marvel of the Mars Curiosity Rover. This lit a spark inside me that, over the years, engulfed into a deep excitement and interest in technology and innovation. My passion has led me to explore and study the ways we humans used and continue to apply the rules of the world to achieve great things. I discovered that science and mathematics are the keys to a functioning universe, and represent the tools and knowledge needed to understand how things interact. With these tools, it is my goal to advance human sustainability, security, health, and well-being in an effort to improve our world.

My pursuit and quest for knowledge have steered me in multiple directions, including undertaking a research semester. I explored the use of machine learning and artificial intelligence to reduce the impacts of neurodevelopmental disorders. I immersed myself in the design and development of key mechanical, electrical, and software systems for a zero-emission race vehicle. And as I progressed through my undergraduate studies, technology and innovation have dominated the many self-directed, multidisciplinary engineering projects showcased on this website!

Aside from engineering, I am enthusiastic about sports, adventure, and my community. I especially enjoy basketball and Formula 1 as both sports require skill, strategy, and ingenuity. In my off-time, I look for every opportunity to travel and explore different cultures, perspectives, foods, and languages. Serving and giving back to my community through regular volunteering is meaningful, rewarding, and fundamental to my values and allows me to contribute towards a strong civil society.
